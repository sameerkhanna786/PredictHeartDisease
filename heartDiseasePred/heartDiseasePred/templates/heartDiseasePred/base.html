Parameter Info

Information and descriptions about the various parameters. All information found here is from Harvard Health, Wikipedia, and HeartOrg.

  • Age: Normally as you age there is a higher likelihood of having heart disease. However, since if you need to see a cardiologist when you are younger, there is a greater likelihood that you have heart disease as opposed to having something else, we see the opposite correlation than expected!
  • Sex: Usually men are more likely to get heart disease by certain age than females; women generally develop heart disease 10 years after men do. However, diabetes, which we measure by asking about your fasting blood sugar, erases that advantage.
  • Chest Pain: Chest Pain is a symptom that is usually indicative of heart disease.
  • Resting blood pressure: High Blood Pressure is a symptom that is usually indicative of heart disease.
  • Serum Cholestrol: High Cholestrol is a symptom that is usually indicative of heart disease.
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: High Fasting Blood Sugar can be indicative of many things, including diabetes. Having diabetes increases your risk for heart disease.
  • Resting electrocardiograph: An electrocardiograph, ECG or EKG for short, measures the electric signals your heart uses to keep rhythm. Doctors use ECGs in order to assess proper heart function.
  • Maximum Heart Rate: A high heart rate can be indicative of underlying issues.
  • Exercise induced angina: Chest pain during excercise can be indicative of underlying issues.
  • ST depression: It is often a sign of myocardial ischemia, of which coronary insufficiency is a major cause.
  • Slope of the peak exercise ST segment: In 1987, Dr. Linden and cohorts found that slope can be used to determine the presence of myocardial ischemia.
  • Number of major vessels colored: This is a direct assessment of myocardial ischemia.
  • Thal: Thal, short for thallium stress test, is a nuclear imaging test that shows how well blood flows into your heart while you're exercising or at rest. This test is also called a cardiac or nuclear stress test.
